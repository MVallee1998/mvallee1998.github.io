Mathieu Vallée

Ph.D.

I am now a postdoc at Université libre de Bruxelles in the Algorithm team, supervised by Jean Cardinal.

I obtained my Ph.D. in Computer Science and Mathematics (September 2025) at the LIPN (Laboratoire d'Informatique de Paris Nord), at the University of Sorbonne Paris Nord in France, supervised by Roland Grappe and Bruno Vallette. My Ph.D. subject is "Cones, fans, and wedges: combinatorial and geometrical perpectives". It gathers combinatorial optimization, polyhedral geometry, and toric geometry.

I teach at the IUT Villetaneuse.

Research

Toric topology

During my Master degree, I took a year off to focus on research with Suyoung Choi, with whom I undertook my bachelor degree internship. Our research is mainly focused on the study of toric manifolds, and the combinatorics that appear from them. The tools used here range from (binary) matroid theory to PL-spheres together with algebraic geometry and Groebner bases.

Box-integrality and the integer decomposition property

Box-integrality is a property of some integer polyhedron to remain integer after a truncation with any integer box. Integer decomposition properties is the study of integer points in convex polyhedra and, more precisely, of how to find "minimal" generators of these integer points. I study integer decomposition properties of polyhedra satisfying box-integrality. For this subject, I use plenty of discrete geometry such as triangulation of polyhedron, box-total dual integrality, and a class of matrices that generalizes totally unimodular matrices: totally equimodular matrices.
